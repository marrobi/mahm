About U&E blood tests

Understanding what the test involves and why it's important for your care.

What does U&E stand for?

U&E stands for Urea and Electrolytes. This blood test measures several important chemicals in your blood that help doctors understand how well your kidneys are working.

What the test measures

Component What it shows
Urea Waste product filtered by kidneys
Creatinine Another waste product that indicates kidney function
Sodium Salt levels in your blood
Potassium Important for heart and muscle function

Why it's important for BP medication

  • Some BP medications can affect how your kidneys work
  • ACE inhibitors and ARBs can sometimes raise potassium levels
  • Diuretics can affect sodium and potassium balance
  • Regular monitoring ensures your medication remains safe
What happens if results are abnormal?

Don't worry - this is why we monitor

If any levels are outside the normal range:

  • Your GP will review the results
  • They may adjust your medication dose
  • Sometimes a different medication is more suitable
  • Repeat tests may be arranged
  • Most changes are easily manageable

The monitoring system is designed to catch any issues early, when they're easiest to address.

What to expect during the test

  1. Registration and check-in (2-3 minutes)
  2. A small blood sample taken from your arm (1-2 minutes)
  3. Brief rest period if needed
  4. Results sent directly to your GP and My BP app
Information:

Good to know: The blood test is quick and straightforward. Most people find it no more uncomfortable than a small pinch.

Frequency of testing

  • When starting medication: Test after 1-2 weeks
  • After dose increases: Test after 4-6 weeks
  • Routine monitoring: Every 6-12 months
  • If problems found: More frequent monitoring until stable
