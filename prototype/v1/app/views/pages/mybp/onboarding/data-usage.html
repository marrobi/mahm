How we use your information

Learn how My BP collects, uses, and protects your health information.

Information:

Quality monitoring: All system interactions are logged to ensure safety and continuously improve your care.

Information we collect

Blood pressure readings

Your blood pressure measurements and how they were taken - used by our monitoring system to track your condition and check readings are accurate.

24-hour monitoring results

Results from extended monitoring - used by our diagnostic system to help confirm if you have high blood pressure for your GP.

Medicines and side effects

Your medicines, how well you take them, and any side effects - used by our medicine management system to help find the right treatment and support you.

Lifestyle and cultural preferences

Your lifestyle, diet, and cultural preferences - used by our lifestyle support system to give you personalised advice that fits with your life and culture.

Treatment choices

Your treatment choices and what matters to you - used by our decision support system to help you make informed choices about your care.

Blood test results

Kidney and liver function results - used by our safety monitoring system to make sure your medicines are safe and working properly.

Warning signs and symptoms

Warning signs and urgent symptoms you report - used by our safety alert system to quickly identify when you need urgent medical help.

Background information

Your background information and any barriers to healthcare - used by our care coordination system to personalise your care and ensure fair access to services.

How we use your information

Information: Your healthcare

  • Give you personalised care: Follow NHS guidelines to recommend the right treatment for you
  • Keep you safe: Monitor for warning signs and alert your doctor when needed
  • Coordinate your care: Work with your GP and other healthcare services
  • Support you: Provide advice that fits your culture, language, and lifestyle
  • Improve the service: Learn what works best to help future patients
  • Help your doctor: Provide clear information to support their decisions

Anonymised data for improvement

Information:

We use anonymised population-level data to improve the service and reduce health inequalities across different communities. Your personal information is never included.

Anonymised data helps us:

  • Monitor health inequality reduction across ethnic groups and socioeconomic areas
  • Improve system performance and safety protocols
  • Enhance cultural appropriateness of lifestyle support
  • Optimise resource allocation in underserved communities
  • Research effectiveness of AI-supported care coordination
What does anonymised mean?

Anonymised data means:

  • All personal identifiers are permanently removed using NHS Digital standards
  • It cannot be linked back to you as an individual
  • It's used only for population health research and service improvement
  • It helps identify patterns that can improve care for everyone
  • It supports our mission to reduce health inequalities

Your rights

You have important rights about how your information is used. If you need help with any of these, contact our Data Protection Officer or speak to your GP practice.

  • Access: Request a copy of your information held in My BP
  • Correct: Update incorrect information in your care records
  • Delete: Request removal of your data (subject to clinical safety requirements)
  • Withdraw: Stop using My BP at any time and return to standard GP care
  • Complain: Contact NHS Digital, your GP practice, or the ICO
  • Transparency: Request explanations of system recommendations from your GP
Contact us about your data rights

Clinical oversight and safety

Information:

Your GP remains in charge: Your GP is always responsible for your care and makes the final decisions about your treatment. They can override any system recommendations at any time.

My BP follows strict safety standards:

  • All recommendations follow NICE clinical guidelines for hypertension
  • Your healthcare team can access and review all system recommendations
  • Clinical safety officers monitor the system for any issues
  • Regular safety reviews ensure the system works as intended
  • If you have any urgent symptoms, you should always contact your GP or call 999

For more information, see the NHS Digital privacy notice and My BP clinical governance framework.
