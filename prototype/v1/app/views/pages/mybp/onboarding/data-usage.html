Data Usage Statement

Overview of how we collect, use, and protect your information in My BP.

Information:

Clinical governance: All AI agent interactions are logged for quality monitoring, safety oversight, and continuous improvement of care delivery.

Data we collect by agent type

Types of data collected by each AI agent in My BP
Data type Collecting agent Purpose
Blood pressure readings & measurement quality BP Measurement Agent Monitor condition, validate measurements, coordinate community monitoring
24-hour ABPM data & diagnostic evidence Diagnosing Agent Confirm hypertension diagnosis, provide evidence packs for GP approval
Medication history, adherence patterns, side effects Titration Agent, Medication Adherence Agent Optimize treatment, improve adherence, prevent complications
Lifestyle factors, cultural preferences, dietary habits Lifestyle Agent Provide culturally-appropriate interventions, reduce health inequalities
Treatment preferences, decision rationale, values Shared Decision-Making Agent Support informed choices, improve treatment satisfaction
Laboratory results (U&E, liver function, lipids) Monitoring Agent Ensure medication safety, prevent complications
Emergency symptoms, risk indicators, escalation triggers Red Flag Agent Immediate safety monitoring, emergency escalation
Demographics, socioeconomic factors, access barriers Orchestrating Agent Coordinate care, address health inequalities, personalize pathways

How we use your data

Information: Direct care purposes

  • Clinical decision support: Generate NICE guideline-compliant recommendations
  • Safety monitoring: Continuous monitoring with immediate escalation protocols
  • Care coordination: Orchestrate multiple agents for comprehensive care
  • Health inequality reduction: Provide culturally-appropriate, accessible interventions
  • Quality improvement: Monitor agent performance and clinical outcomes
  • GP support: Provide evidence packs for complex clinical decisions

Patient category data management

Your data usage varies by your patient category:

  • Category A (Known hypertension): Full treatment management, medication optimization, monitoring
  • Category B (Normotensive surveillance): Periodic monitoring, lifestyle support, preventive interventions
  • Category C (Newly detected elevated BP): Diagnostic support, ABPM coordination, GP approval workflows

Anonymized data for improvement

Information:

We use anonymized population-level data to improve the service and reduce health inequalities across different communities. Your personal information is never included.

Anonymized data helps us:

  • Monitor health inequality reduction across ethnic groups and socioeconomic areas
  • Improve AI agent performance and safety protocols
  • Enhance cultural appropriateness of lifestyle interventions
  • Optimize resource allocation in underserved communities
  • Research effectiveness of AI-supported care coordination
What does anonymized mean?

Anonymized data means:

  • All personal identifiers are permanently removed using NHS Digital standards
  • It cannot be linked back to you as an individual
  • It's used only for population health research and service improvement
  • It helps identify patterns that can improve care for everyone
  • It supports our mission to reduce health inequalities

Your rights

  • Access: Request a copy of your data across all AI agents
  • Correct: Update incorrect information in your care records
  • Delete: Request removal of your data (subject to clinical safety requirements)
  • Withdraw: Stop using My BP at any time and return to standard GP care
  • Complain: Contact NHS Digital, your GP practice, or the ICO
  • Transparency: Request explanations of AI agent recommendations

Clinical governance and oversight

Information:

Human oversight: All AI agent recommendations are subject to clinical governance frameworks with GP escalation protocols when needed.

For more information, see the NHS Digital privacy notice and My BP clinical governance framework.
